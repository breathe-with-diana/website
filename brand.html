For you, Diana · who you are

Who you are & what you offer.

These are the words underneath everything — your website, your captions, your bio. They’re built from what you said in our sessions. Read each one and check: does this sound like me?

Tap “Looks good” on anything that feels right. If a word is slightly off, type the version that’s more you — that’s the most useful thing you can do here. You can write in Spanish if it’s easier.
1 · Who you help

For people whose life looks right on the outside but feels empty inside — and who are ready to stop avoiding it.

The work is coming home to your body: making peace with what’s inside, instead of looking outside for the next fix, distraction, or way to numb it.

2 · What you offer — two doors
  • Door 1 — calm Simple breathing tools for everyday calm. An easy first step, free to learn.
  • Door 2 — deep Deeper rebirthing sessions that move stuck emotion and bring you back to yourself.

People can start gentle and go deeper when they’re ready. More “doors” can be added later.

On money: it’s never the promise. Inner peace first — and abundance may follow, but you’ll never sell it as a guarantee.

3 · What makes you you

“I built the life I was told would make me happy and still felt empty. I left it. Rebirthing is how I found my way back — now I guide others through it.”

Your difference isn’t a job title — it’s that you lived this yourself, and you guide as an equal who’s been there, not a guru above it.

Note: we’re not leading with “Colombian” or where you live on the English side — those belong in your story when it fits, and feel more at home on your Spanish presence. Tell us if you’d rather they’re more (or less) visible.

4 · Your four words

The feeling every post should pass — and what each one keeps you away from:

  • Grounded body-first, real — never floaty or “high vibration”
  • Honest true about the work, even when it’s hard — never preachy or savior-y
  • Warm human and equal — never clinical, never guru-from-above
  • Slow unhurried — never hype or “last chance”

In Spanish: Aterrizada · Honesta · Cálida · Lenta.

5 · What you’ll never do

Often clearer than what you will do. You’ll never:

  • Promise to heal or fix anyone — not even “in X weeks”
  • Talk down like a guru — you share the tools that helped you, as an equal, and never tell people to follow you or buy from you
  • Run a messy hobby account — it stays focused; no showing-off, no random private snapshots, no travel-as-content unless there’s a real message in it
  • Post money / “manifest abundance” hooks — abundance only ever as a quiet by-product of inner peace
  • Use heavy filters or fake-candid shots — the whole point is the real you
6 · Look & feel
  • Colours soft, warm beige base + one warm accent, with ochre as a rare eye-catcher
  • Photos mostly real and unfiltered — you, no makeup, easy to relate to — with the occasional professional shot

(The logos are a separate review — that’s page 3.)

7 · What you’ll post

Five types to start with — all things you said you like:

  • Breath Lab one technique at a time (video of you, or an animation with your voice)
  • Reflections a nature photo + a short, honest thought
  • My Story short episodes, looking back
  • A session with me what it’s actually like, so a stranger knows what to expect
  • Client reviews real words from people you’ve worked with

Reviews show the person’s name, photo and Instagram (with their permission) and stay in their own language — that’s what makes them feel real. The “explain the science” idea is parked for later; quotes only now and then.

8 · Languages

We build in English first, then make a Spanish and later a Russian version of the same site and content — each one re-told in that language, not a dead word-for-word translation.

We won’t use the word “bilingual” as a label anywhere — speaking several languages just shows up naturally in the site having those versions.
