For you, Diana · gathering reviews

A simple way to collect reviews.

Real client words are some of the most powerful content you have — and their main home is Instagram, where each one becomes an on-brand review post (the website's three review cards pull from the same place). This is how we'd gather them — and keep gathering them — without you copying and pasting anything. One small form you send to a client after a session; their words, photo, and permission all land in one tidy list we draw from, for Instagram first and the website too.

On each block below: tap “Looks good” if you’re happy, or type a note if you’d change something. Then hit Send feedback.
1 · The idea, in one breath

After a session, you send a client one short link (or show them a little square code to scan). They fill in a few things on their own phone — no app, no account, nothing to download.

Everything they write drops into one private list only you and Daniel can see. When it’s time to post, we pick a lovely one and it becomes an Instagram review post (and a card on your website) — their permission was already given on the form, so there’s nothing to chase afterwards.

Mockup — the real form is built once you’re happy with the idea.

Three quiet but important touches: (1) the photo and Instagram are gently pushed as “needed” — a real face and profile are what make a stranger trust a review, so the form leans on people to add them, with a small “I’d rather not” escape so you don’t lose the whole review. Everyone’s shown how they’ll appear (“Maria, 34 · @handle”) so it feels safe. (2) The “what could I have done better?” answer is kept private — only for you, never on a post. (3) The permission tick is its own thing: if someone leaves it unticked, you still get their words and honest feedback, you just won’t post them.

3 · How you’ll use it, after every session

The form staying warm is about how and when you send it — personally, in your voice, at the right moment — not the form itself:

🌱
The day after is just aftercare — never the ask. The first day or two can still be a lot: physical tiredness, emotion, things still settling. So your first message is only a check-in — “how are you feeling today?” — no link, no request.
💬
A few days later, once they feel grounded, send the form. A WhatsApp or DM in your own words — “It was beautiful breathing with you. Now that it’s had time to settle, if you feel like sharing a few words, here 🤍 [link]”. Never a mass email. (Same timing whether the session was in person or online.)
📇
You already have their contact. You take everyone’s email & phone at booking anyway — for the aftercare check-in — so you can always send the link later. Nobody fills anything on the spot. (A QR code at the end of an in-person session is just an optional extra.)
🗂️
Everything lands in one central place. From there the review tiles for Instagram are generated automatically — no copying, no design work. All that’s left is a quick human glance to approve before it goes out.
🌍
Spanish version later. Same form, in Spanish, when you’re ready — both feed the same one list. (English first, like the website.)
