Canva, part one

Setting up Canva

A short, one time setup. Most of this I do for you. You mainly need to create the account and accept an invite.

  1. Create a free Canva account with your brand email. You
  2. I load your brand kit: your logo, your colours, your fonts, and your no dash rule, so everything you make matches the website. Daniel
  3. I build your templates: a quote card, a swipe, and a clip caption style, all ready to fill in. Daniel
  4. I upload your photo and clip library so your own images are right there when you need them, sorted into folders. Daniel
  5. You accept the invite and we open one template together so it feels familiar. You

Canva, part two

Using Canva, the five moves

This is the whole skill. Five moves, and you can make any post. You always begin from a template, never from nothing.

  1. Open a template. Pick "quote card" or "swipe" from your folder. It is already designed.
  2. Swap the photo. Drag one of your own images in, or pick from the library. The layout holds itself.
  3. Change the words. Click the text, paste the line your GPT wrote. The font and colour are already set.
  4. Glance for brand. Does it feel like you, calm and warm? If yes, move on. If not, try another photo.
  5. Export. Press share, download, and it is ready for the scheduler. Done.
The only rule: if you ever feel like you are "designing from scratch", stop. That is a sign the template is missing, and that is my job to fix, not yours.

Our first time

The first session, post by post

We do this side by side. The goal is not three perfect posts. The goal is for you to feel, in your own hands, that this is doable.

Post 1 · we make it together

We pick the easiest format you hearted. Your GPT writes it. We build it in Canva or CapCut together, start to finish, and load it into the scheduler. You watch how light it is.

Post 2 · you lead, I sit beside you

A different format or spark. This time your hands are on the keyboard and I only help when you ask. We find the bumpy parts and smooth them.

Post 3 · you try on your own, then we look

You make one by yourself, in your own time, and we review it together after. Whatever felt hard becomes the next thing I set up for you, so it is easier next time.

We learn along the way. Every time you get stuck, that is information, not failure. It tells us exactly what to make more automatic.
