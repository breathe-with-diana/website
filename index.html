What a session is like

Honest about the work — before you ever book.

1

We talk first

Every session opens with a conversation — what brought you here, what feels stuck, anything I should know. No surprises.

2

You breathe

A continuous, connected breathing pattern I guide you through. You lie down; I hold the space and pace. This is the heart of rebirthing.

3

Something moves

Emotion often surfaces — tears, shaking, sound, sometimes a wave of heat or cold. This is the stuck weight leaving the body. It's normal, and it's the point.

4

We land

We close slowly, and I stay with you afterward — plus follow-up support in the days after, because the morning after is where the real shift shows up.

The honest part

This isn't a relaxing spa breath. A session is around 2–3 hours, and it can be intense — most people leave tired, like a weight just passed through them.

I'll never promise to fix you or heal you. What I can offer is a safe, steady space to meet what you've been avoiding — and the tools to hold yourself once you have.

Breathwork isn't suitable for everyone. If you're pregnant or have a heart, respiratory, or certain mental-health conditions, we'll talk it through on a free call first.

My story
I built the life I was told would make me happy — and still felt empty. So I left it.

I'm Diana. I'm Colombian, and I trained in rebirthing — a specific lineage of conscious-connected breathwork, not generic “breathwork.” For years I did everything right on paper, and quietly felt like I was missing myself the whole time.

The morning I knew I was leaving my old life, I needed to actually leave it. I left a job I was good at, a marriage I had built, and the identity that came with both — not because they were wrong, but because they had stopped fitting.

Rebirthing breathwork is how I found my way back to my own body. Not in a weekend, and not without some hard mornings. I work with the women I used to be — people who built the life they were supposed to want, arrived, and felt the quiet emptiness underneath. Now I guide them through the same work, as someone who lived it, not as a guru above it.

Do I need any experience?

None at all. I guide you through every breath. Most people who come have never done breathwork before.

Will I feel calm afterward?

Sometimes — but calm isn't the promise. Rebirthing tends to move emotion rather than soothe it. Many people feel tired and tender right after, and lighter the next day.

Is this therapy?

No. It's a complement to inner work, not a replacement for therapy or medical care. If you're in treatment, keep your therapist in the loop.

Can we do it online?

Yes. Online sessions work well over video once we've had a short call to set up your space safely. [Diana to confirm online offering.]
