For you, Diana · saying thank-you

A little thank-you for sharing.

When someone trusts you with the people they love — sending a friend, a sister, a partner your way — that’s the highest compliment there is, and it’s also how a practice like yours quietly grows. This is a small, warm way to thank the people who do that: when a few of the people they’ve sent have come for a session, their next one is your gift. It’s kept completely separate from reviews on purpose — a review is a gift from the heart and should never be paid for, so this never touches it.

On each block below: tap “Looks good” if you’re happy, or type a note if you’d change something. Then hit Send feedback.
1 · The idea, in one breath

This is not a loud “refer a friend” deal — no codes, no posters, nothing salesy. It’s a personal thank-you you offer the people you’ve genuinely connected with: “if you send people my way, after a few of them I’ll gift you a session.”

It works quietly in the background. You don’t chase anyone; you simply notice when someone has sent three people who came — and you give them a session as a thank-you.

2 · How you’ll know who to thank

The only thing this needs is one gentle line when a new person books: “Did someone send you? Who?” That’s the whole system — no referral codes, no special links, nothing for anyone to download.

When the answer comes in, you note it next to that person in the same central list your reviews live in — just its own little tab, kept apart from the review words so the two never mix. When a name reaches three sessions sent, you’ll see it, and you can reach out with the gift.

3 · The thank-you, in plain terms
  • The gift3 people who complete a private session with you → 1 session on the house for the person who sent them.
  • “Complete”, not just bookIt counts once the person has actually had their session — so a cancellation or a no-show doesn’t count. Fair, and nothing to keep chasing.
  • It adds up over timeThe count simply keeps growing — there’s no clock, no expiry. Three across a year still earns the thank-you.
  • Yours to giveIt’s always your gesture, in your hands. If someone’s sent two lovely people and you feel moved to thank them already, that’s entirely your call.
One small choice for you: is three the right number — or would you rather it be two, or four? Easy to set to whatever feels right.
4 · How people hear about it

Because this is a warm gesture and not a campaign, it lives mostly in your own words, with a couple of quiet backups so people who’d love to share always know they can:
