Before the day: prep & principles

Two things I need from you, Diana

Locations. Confirm which spots are realistic (a healing space, your home, beach, jungle, waterfall) and, if you can, send a few phone photos of each. Even rough snaps let us plan angles and light instead of guessing on the day.
Wardrobe. Lay out and photograph the outfits you would feel good in. We are planning 3 looks across the day (see below), all in your warm, earthy palette. Show me what you have and I will map outfits to locations.

Shot DNA (applies to every shot)

  • Vertical first. Shoot most things 9:16 for reels and stories. Grab a 4:5 and a wide 16:9 of the strongest setups too, so one moment serves every format.
  • Leave room for text. On portraits and scenery, deliberately frame with empty space beside or above Diana. That negative space is where captions and quotes go later. These plates are gold.
  • Two takes of the key moments. One with eyes to camera (connection), one unaware and candid (calm, in her own world).
  • Clips: slow and loopable. 10 to 20 seconds each, slow deliberate movement, steady or gentle gimbal. A few that loop seamlessly (breath in, breath out, settle).
  • Light. Outdoors, shoot the golden window: first 90 minutes after sunrise or last 90 before sunset. Soft, warm, low sun. Avoid harsh midday overhead light.
  • Capture high-res RAW for photos and the highest quality the camera allows for video, so stills can be pulled from clips.

Wardrobe plan (3 looks)

  • Look A, soft and flowing in cream, bone or oatmeal. For the healing space and home. Intimate, calm, the gentle door.
  • Look B, earthy in terracotta, rust or olive. For the jungle and waterfall. Grounded, deeper, elemental.
  • Look C, light linen in sand or warm white. For the beach. Airy and open.
  • Barefoot wherever it is natural. Minimal jewellery. Capture hair both down and loosely tied.

Props to bring

  • A breathwork mat or blanket, a couple of cushions or bolsters.
  • A few candles, a small ceramic cup, a journal and pen.
  • A light shawl or wrap that moves in the wind (great for beach and waterfall clips).
  • The model: comfortable, palette-friendly clothing, someone at ease being touched gently and guided.
